BTC's History Abstract Conclusion
In 2008 an anonymous person or group under the pseudonym of Satoshi Nakamoto published Bitcoin's white paper, proposing the concept of a decentralized p2p digital cash system that would allow payments to be directly sent to other users without having to utilize a third party such as a financial institution. To avoid the problem of double spending, the transactions are verified using cryptographic proof and recorded in a public ledger referred to as a blockchain.

It is implied that the motive behind the creation of Bitcoin was in response to the global financial crisis between 2007-2008. The inherent problem with fiat currencies is that trust is required to make them work. The central bank must be trusted not to devalue the currency, yet history has shown us that trust has been often breached.

On January 3, 2009 the genesis block of the Bitcoin network was mined. Embedded in the block is the text "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks." Bitcoin's software was implemented as an open source code.
Since Bitcoin's inception in 2009, it has gained widespread media attention for its exponential growth in perceived value. It has been acclaimed as the future of currency. It has also been criticized for its uses in illegal activity and has been regarded as this century's greatest bubble. This project examines the price of Bitcoin since 2010, and Bitcoin's representation in the media, including The New York Times, Twitter, Reddit, and Google Trends. Weíve analyzed the evolution of global interest and sentiment about Bitcoin, to determine the extent to which that is reflective or even predictive of Bitcoinís price. Data is charted by time and geography, and categorized by sentiment. The price of Bitcoin is compared with gold and the S&P 500.

We find that global interest in Bitcoin has peaked at times of heavy fluctuation in its price, and increased interest can be seen in individual countries when experiencing financial unsteadiness or upheaval. Bitcoin has the potential to outperform gold and the market, but timing and patience are key. Bitcoin is mainly driven by speculation, and monitoring media activity and sentiment can help inform an investors decision.
